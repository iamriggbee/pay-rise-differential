Rule of Thumb
A 0.1% higher salary on £30,000, with the gap growing with CPI and generating matched pension contributions, is worth about £5,800 over 25 years.
ASSUMPTIONS — Starting salary £30,000. Future pay rises are CPI-only, applied each year continuously across the 25-year career. Extra pension contribution: 0.1% employee + 0.1% employer = 0.2% of that year's salary, compounding at the selected pension growth rate until retirement. The pension contribution rate shown in the controls is for context only — the extra contribution is always exactly 0.1%+0.1% of the pay gap. All figures are nominal and gross — income tax, NI, and pension tax relief are not modelled. "Without compounding" assumes the annual £ gap stays fixed at its year-1 value throughout the career. The pension pot is a fund value at retirement, not an annuity.
