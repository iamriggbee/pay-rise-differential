What this measures: All figures show the marginal difference caused purely by the 0.1% pay gap — not your total salary or pension, just the extra generated by being on a 0.1% higher salary base.
NATS Defined Benefit Scheme (CAAPS): Accrual rate 1/58th. Your pension at retirement = (total service years ÷ 58) × final pensionable earnings. The 0.1% gap means your final pensionable earnings are 0.1% higher (after CPI compounding over years to retirement), so your entire accrued pension — across all service years — is 0.1% larger. In payment, the pension is uprated by CPI each year.
